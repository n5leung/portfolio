Title: Brand Identity Design: Logo & Poster for a Client

Project type: Poster & Icon Design

Tools: Adobe Illustrator

⟡ Description ⟡

For this project, I designed a cohesive logo and poster for a client whose interests include coding, robotics, and minimalist digital design. I built the visual identity around the concept of a computer interface, using a webpage layout for the poster and a folder-based symbol for the logo. Both pieces integrate puzzle imagery, window frames, and structured shapes to express the client’s journey in design and technology.

Using Adobe Illustrator, I developed a clean, modern visual style with simple rectangles, layered window effects, and a cool blue palette inspired by digital interfaces. The final designs blend clarity, minimalism, and personality while responding directly to the client’s preferences and feedback.

⟡ Reflection ⟡

1. Concept Development & Client Understanding

My initial brainstorming focused on the client’s keywords: journey, coding, robotics, minimalist, design. I explored visual metaphors like puzzle pieces, paths leading to doors, and technical icons to represent these themes. Through feedback discussions, I learned that the client preferred a clean and simple direction, which guided me to refine my concept using the folder icon and computer window elements as the core visual identity. This helped create a clear connection between the logo and poster, making the brand feel unified.

2. Process & Tools

I used Adobe Illustrator as the main tool for constructing the layout and graphics. Simple rectangles and linework created the webpage aesthetic, while layered window shapes helped achieve a subtle 3D perspective. I experimented with different fonts for the initials and selected the one that best supported a minimalist tech style. My color palette centered on blue, reflecting digital environments like Windows interfaces. Throughout the process, I relied on YouTube tutorials and adjusted ideas when I faced technical challenges, reinforcing my adaptability and problem-solving approach.

3. Visual Decisions & Style

The poster uses a playlist-style webpage with the client’s initials as the cover art, connecting the idea of a “creative journey.” Elements like the mini three-color palette, coding window, and top-left folder icons were added to reflect the client’s hobbies and strengths. To maintain balance, I placed elements evenly on both sides of the composition. The entire project highlights the use of shape and minimalist structure to express clarity and professionalism.

4. Key Learnings

This project helped me strengthen my ability to interpret client needs and translate feedback into cleaner design decisions. I also practiced experimenting with perspective, layers, and interface-inspired compositions. With more time, I would explore alternative webpage layouts or new color palettes to expand the design direction and deepen the connection to the client’s brief.
